My name is Emily Zeng. I'm a first year student from the University of Waterloo. I study Mechatronics Engineering, which is an interdisciplinary program combining software, hardware, and mechanical skills. I am passionate about Machine Learning and Robotics.

About

More About Me.

In my last internship, I was responsible for helping develop a course for machine learning. Using sci-kit learn, I made a facial recognition program with python. It used principal component analysis to train a model to recognize faces with a precision of 85% at maximum. I also made a MEAN stack website which will be used as a template for adding on machine learning models, such as for related products or categorization. Both those projects will be used as a case study in the machine learning course.

I have also been part of the Scarlett Robotics Team from 2014 to 2016, which greatly developed my appreciation for the importance of prototyping and testing, as well as a love for robotics. I participated predominately in the mechanical and programming subgroups. In the 2016 Stronghold competition, I designed and prototyped the mechanical arm used to shoot "boulders".

Beyond that, I also enjoy being active in the community. I have been the co-founder of the Scarlett Tutoring Society, and the Communications Advisor in the Principal's Advisory Committee. I also love to draw, especially sketches with charcoal (check them out here). In my spare time, I like to play Dungeons and Dragons, as well as Table Tennis.
